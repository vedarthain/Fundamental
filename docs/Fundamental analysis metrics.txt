Here’s a comprehensive list of the most important fundamental analysis ratios and metrics used to evaluate any stock. These are commonly used by investors, analysts, hedge funds, and quantitative stock-ranking systems.

⸻

1. Valuation Ratios

Used to determine whether a stock is expensive or cheap.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
P/E Ratio	Price / EPS	Valuation based on earnings
Forward P/E	Price / Forward EPS	Future valuation
PEG Ratio	P/E / Earnings Growth	Growth-adjusted valuation
Price to Book (P/B)	Price / Book Value	Asset valuation
Price to Sales (P/S)	Market Cap / Revenue	Useful for low-profit firms
EV/EBITDA	Enterprise Value / EBITDA	Capital-structure-neutral valuation
EV/Sales	Enterprise Value / Revenue	Growth companies
EV/EBIT	EV / EBIT	Operational valuation
Dividend Yield	Dividend / Price	Income return
Earnings Yield	EPS / Price	Inverse of P/E
Free Cash Flow Yield	FCF / Market Cap	Cash generation efficiency
Market Cap / Sales	Market Cap / Revenue	Scale valuation
Graham Number	√(22.5 × EPS × BVPS)	Intrinsic value estimate

⸻

2. Profitability Ratios

Measures business quality and earning efficiency.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
ROE	Net Income / Equity	Shareholder return
ROCE	EBIT / Capital Employed	Capital efficiency
ROA	Net Income / Assets	Asset efficiency
ROIC	NOPAT / Invested Capital	True capital efficiency
Gross Margin	Gross Profit / Revenue	Pricing power
EBITDA Margin	EBITDA / Revenue	Operating efficiency
EBIT Margin	EBIT / Revenue	Core profitability
Net Profit Margin	Net Profit / Revenue	Bottom-line profitability
Operating Margin	Operating Income / Revenue	Operational performance
Cash Flow Margin	Operating CF / Revenue	Cash profitability
Incremental ROCE	ΔEBIT / ΔCapital	Growth quality

⸻

3. Growth Ratios

Measures business expansion.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
Revenue Growth	Revenue change YoY	Sales expansion
EPS Growth	EPS change YoY	Profit growth
EBITDA Growth	EBITDA change	Operating growth
Profit CAGR	CAGR of profits	Long-term consistency
Sales CAGR	CAGR of sales	Growth sustainability
Book Value CAGR	CAGR of BVPS	Wealth creation
Free Cash Flow Growth	FCF growth	Cash expansion
Asset Growth	Asset increase	Business scale
Dividend Growth Rate	Dividend CAGR	Shareholder returns

⸻

4. Liquidity Ratios

Measures short-term financial strength.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
Current Ratio	Current Assets / Current Liabilities	Short-term safety
Quick Ratio	(CA − Inventory) / CL	Liquid strength
Cash Ratio	Cash / Current Liabilities	Immediate liquidity
Working Capital Ratio	Current Assets − Current Liabilities	Operational liquidity

⸻

5. Solvency / Debt Ratios

Measures long-term financial risk.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
Debt to Equity	Total Debt / Equity	Leverage risk
Debt to Assets	Debt / Assets	Financial dependency
Interest Coverage	EBIT / Interest Expense	Debt servicing ability
Net Debt / EBITDA	Net Debt / EBITDA	Debt burden
Debt Service Coverage Ratio	Operating Income / Debt Obligations	Repayment capacity
Financial Leverage Ratio	Assets / Equity	Balance sheet leverage
Long-term Debt to Capital	LTD / (Debt + Equity)	Capital structure

⸻

6. Cash Flow Ratios

Critical for quality investing.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
Operating Cash Flow Ratio	OCF / Current Liabilities	Cash strength
Free Cash Flow	OCF − Capex	True cash generation
FCF Margin	FCF / Revenue	Cash efficiency
Cash Conversion Ratio	OCF / EBITDA	Earnings quality
CFO to Net Income	OCF / Net Income	Profit quality
Capex to Sales	Capex / Revenue	Reinvestment intensity
FCF to Net Income	FCF / Net Income	Earnings sustainability

⸻

7. Efficiency Ratios

Measures operational efficiency.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
Asset Turnover	Revenue / Assets	Asset productivity
Inventory Turnover	COGS / Inventory	Inventory efficiency
Receivables Turnover	Revenue / Receivables	Collection efficiency
Payables Turnover	Purchases / Payables	Supplier management
Working Capital Turnover	Revenue / Working Capital	Capital usage
Fixed Asset Turnover	Revenue / Fixed Assets	Infrastructure efficiency
Cash Conversion Cycle	DSO + DIO − DPO	Working capital efficiency

⸻

8. Shareholder Ratios

Important for equity investors.

Ratio	Formula	Purpose
EPS	Net Income / Shares	Earnings per share
BVPS	Equity / Shares	Book value per share
Dividend Payout Ratio	Dividend / Earnings	Shareholder reward
Retention Ratio	Retained Earnings / Net Income	Growth reinvestment
Buyback Yield	Buybacks / Market Cap	Capital return
Total Shareholder Yield	Dividend + Buyback Yield	Total return
Dilution Rate	Share Increase %	Ownership dilution

⸻

9. Quality Ratios

Used in advanced quant investing.

Ratio	Purpose
Piotroski F-Score	Financial strength

Beneish M-Score	Earnings manipulation detection
Sloan Ratio	Earnings quality
Accrual Ratio	Profit reliability
Earnings Stability	Consistency of profits
Cash Earnings Ratio	Cash-based profitability
